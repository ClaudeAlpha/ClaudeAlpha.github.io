These are 3 of the games that I have developed either on my own or as part of a team during my time at university so far:

  1. Dash Royale

    2. This game was developed by myself during the first year and I deem it to be my best piece of that year.

    • This game is for 2 players.
    • It has a versus mode and a cooperative mode.
    • In versus mode players have to fight each other in an arena surrounded by red lasers and the one that depletes their battery first loses.
    • To deplete their battery by 1 line, a player has to touch the laser, either by dashing into it themselves or by getting pushed into it by the other player.
    • A player charges up their dash by pressing Up->Right->Down->Left, which charges 1 line. This has to be done 4 times to be fully charged.
    • At full charge a player can dash away towards the opponent, pushing them into the red laser if they hit them or end up into the laser themselves if the opponent dodges their attack.
    • In cooperative mode the blue player has to protect the green player from getting hit by boulders.
    • There's 3 boulders, a small square, a small rectangle and a giant square. The blue player can use their charge to dash away the giant square as well as the little ones.
    • The green player however has to use their dash to dodge incoming boulders which weren't dashed away by the blue player.
    • The green player can be hit by a boulder 5 times before game over.

  2. Bouncy Bullets

    3. This game was developed by myself during the second year and I deem it to be my best piece of that year.

    • This is a 2D platformer shooter and it's for 2 players only.
    • It has a versus mode where 2 players fight each other and the first to kill the other one 10 times wins.
    • During gameplay, players can use doors and gravity to move between platforms and their rifles to shoot the other players.
    • The bullets will bounce off the walls and they will disappear after bouncing 10 times or after hitting one of the players.
    • The game also contains a script file where users can set their resolution, maximum bounces, maximum points to win, bullet and player speed, as well as gravity.
    • After each round, the names and kills of the players are recorded into a highscore file which will display the latest 6 matches in the menu.

  3. Kubay

    4. Programmers: Claudiu Moldovan & Sam Chandler

    Artists: Tony, Stephano, Bashir & Mohammed

    • This a singleplayer open world survival game.
    • The aim of the game is to survive for as long as possible by keeping track of your health, hunger and stamina.
    • You can harvest blackberries and blueberries from bushes and you can also obtain raw meat from roaming elks that you have to run after to kill.
    • You can use the cooking pots scattered around the map to cook your raw meat and obtain cooked meat, which restores a lot more hunger.
    • Both elks and tribesmen respawn in a random location some time after being killed, and all berry bushes regrow their berries after 10 minutes.
    • You are also constantly being chased by 4 tribesmen who want to kill you for tresspassing, they are faster than your normal speed, so you have to either fight or flee.
    • Your health will not regenerate while your hunger is below 85%, so you constantly have to keep track and keep your character fed after a battle or chase.
