These are 3 of the best games that I have developed either on my own or as part of a team during my time at university:

  1. Chase Escape

    2. This game was developed by myself using the Unity Engine during the third year of university.

      This game features:

    • A chaser enemy that runs after the player, avoids obstacles and jumps over pits.
    • An ambusher enemy that hides and breaks out of a large box when you get too close and attacks you with energy balls.
    • The ability to restart the game from the beginning at any point by pressing R.
    • A playable character that constantly moves forward on its own and your entire task is to press space to jump over obstacles.
    • A timer and a counter which keeps track of the amount of attempts you take until you win.

  2. Bouncy Bullets

    3. This game was developed by myself during the second year and I deem it to be my best piece of that year.

    • This is a 2D platformer shooter and it's for 2 players only.
    • It has a versus mode where 2 players fight each other and the first to kill the other one 10 times wins.
    • During gameplay, players can use doors and gravity to move between platforms and their rifles to shoot the other players.
    • The bullets will bounce off the walls and they will disappear after bouncing 10 times or after hitting one of the players.
    • The game also contains a script file where users can set their resolution, maximum bounces, maximum points to win, bullet and player speed, as well as gravity.
    • After each round, the names and kills of the players are recorded into a highscore file which will display the latest 6 matches in the menu.

  3. Battlestage

    4. This was my final year project during the third year of university. It doesn't have any gameplay, its purpose is to showcase the

    management systems incorporated, e.g. the inventory system, the levelling system, the saving and loading and the options menu.

    This game features:

    • A shop inventory from where you can buy every item in the game.
    • A player inventory that can have items added and removed from it, as well as automatic alphabetical item sorting.
    • The ability to equip and unequip items, provided you meet their attribute requirement.
    • A levelling system with 4 attributes that impact your health, stamina and amount of items you can equip.
    • An options menu that allows you to choose between 3 screen resolutions, as well as being able to modify sound and music volume.
    • A saving system that allows you to save the game into 1 of 3 slots as well as being able to load the game from any slot.
